    POWER BI

    Sales of Products

    This project analyzes a transactional sales dataset containing information such as Order ID, Product, Quantity Ordered, Price Each, Order Date, and Purchase Address, along with derived attributes like Month, Sales, City, and Hour. Using Power BI, I performed comprehensive data cleaning and transformation, validated the dataset for completeness, and created interactive dashboards to uncover key insights, including sales trends over time, product performance, and city-level sales distribution.

    The dataset was sourced from KeithGalliâ€™s GitHub repository and is also available on Kaggle.

    Data Cleaning and Transformation

    • The sales dataset was thoroughly prepared and standardized to ensure accuracy, consistency, and reliability for analysis and visualization in Power BI.
    • All columns were assigned appropriate data types to support correct calculations and consistent formatting.
    • The dataset schema was optimized by renaming columns for clarity, such as converting the blank column to ID, and by selecting only the relevant columns required for analysis: Order ID, Product, Quantity Ordered, Price Each, Order Date, Sales, and City. All other columns, including ID, Month, Hour, and Purchase Address, were removed to simplify the dataset and focus on analytical needs.
    • A comprehensive data quality check was performed to identify any missing values. All key fields were found to be complete, with no nulls detected, ensuring the dataset is reliable for subsequent analysis and visualization.

    Key Findings

    • The sales dataset generated a total sales value of $34.49M with a total quantity ordered of 209K units.
    • San Francisco contributed the highest sales at $8.26M, while Austin recorded the lowest at $1.82M.
    • Sales peaked in December ($4.61M), while January recorded the lowest sales at $1.82M.
    • The MacBook Pro Laptop is the top-selling product, accounting for 23.3% of total sales.
    • The AAA Batteries (4-pack) generated the lowest sales at 0.27%.
